1. Availability Signals (Time-Bound, Explicit)
Visible intent. These expire automatically.
UI framing: “Availability (optional)”
Open to new full-time roles
Open to contract / consulting work
Hiring / building a team
Looking for a co-founder
Exploring next opportunity (non-urgent)
Rules
Max 1–2 selectable
Expires in 14–30 days
Hidden by default unless enabled
2. Learning & Interest Signals (Orientation)
This is where passive professionals live.
UI framing: “Currently exploring”
AI / machine learning
Developer tooling & platforms
Product strategy & discovery
Data & analytics
Security & privacy
Climate / sustainability
Healthcare / life sciences
Rules
Multi-select
Soft expiration (90-day nudge)
These are not commitments
3. Contribution Signals (Low-Pressure)
Signals generosity without obligation.
UI framing: “Open to”
Mentoring
Advising startups
Reviewing work (design, code, docs)
Speaking or teaching
Informal knowledge sharing
Rules
Soft expiration
No time guarantees
No public “response rate” metrics
4. Perspective & Work Style Signals (Identity-Level)
These replace LinkedIn “headlines.”
UI framing: “How I work”
Builder / hands-on
Systems thinker
Early-stage oriented
Enterprise-scale experience
Product-led mindset
Research-first mindset
Strong opinions, loosely held
Rules
Persistent
Max 2–3 selectable
Rarely changed
Hard Constraints (Very Important)
Selection Limits
Max 5 signals total
Forces intentionality
Prevents profile inflation
No Free Text (v1)
Taxonomy only
You can add freeform later if needed
This keeps search clean and spam low
Example Profiles (This Is the Test)
Passive Senior Engineer
Exploring AI / ML
Systems thinker
Open to mentoring
No job search. Still highly discoverable.
Founder
Hiring / building a team
Early-stage oriented
Advising startups
Clear, calm, high-signal.
Happy-but-Curious Product Leader
Exploring healthcare
Product-led mindset
Speaking or teaching
Zero stigma. Zero pressure.
Why This Taxonomy Works
Covers intent, curiosity, contribution, and identity
No vanity
No performative language
No “thought leadership”
Searchable, filterable, composable
Most importantly:
It gives professionals something meaningful to say
without forcing them to say anything at all.
What NOT to Add in v1 (Resist This)
“Open to relocation”
Seniority labels
Buzzwords (“rockstar”, “ninja”)
Engagement metrics
Industry lists longer than 7–8 items
You can always add later. You can never subtract cleanly.
